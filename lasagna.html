Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish that layers rich flavors and comforting textures into a hearty meal. At its core, lasagna features sheets of tender pasta layered with a savory mixture of ground meat, onions, garlic, and tomato sauce, creating a robust base. Between these layers, creamy ricotta cheese or béchamel sauce is spread generously, complemented by a blend of grated mozzarella and Parmesan that melts into a bubbly, golden topping when baked. Herbs like oregano, basil, and parsley infuse the dish with aromatic notes, making each bite a symphony of flavors. Lasagna is traditionally baked in a casserole dish, allowing the layers to meld together into a cohesive, satisfying entrée.

While the traditional recipe is beloved worldwide, lasagna has evolved into countless variations, reflecting regional and dietary preferences. Vegetable lasagna, for instance, swaps meat for a medley of zucchini, spinach, and mushrooms, appealing to vegetarians. Meanwhile, gluten-free or keto-friendly versions use alternatives like zucchini slices or eggplant in place of pasta. Regardless of the variation, lasagna remains a quintessential comfort food that is perfect for feeding a crowd, freezing for later, or simply enjoying as a family favorite. Its warm, cheesy, and flavorful profile makes it an enduring dish for celebrations and casual dinners alike.

Ingredients

Here is an exact list of ingredients for a lasagna recipe that serves six people:

For the Meat Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 500g (1 pound) ground beef or pork (or a mix of both)
  • 800g (28 ounces) canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • 1/4 cup water (if needed for consistency)

For the Cheese Filling

  • 250g (1 cup) ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley (or 1 teaspoon dried)

For Assembly

  • 9-12 lasagna sheets
  • 300g (3 cups) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese for topping
  • Optional garnish: fresh basil leaves

Steps to Cook Lasagna for 6 People

1. Prepare the Meat Sauce

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
  4. Add the ground beef (or pork) and cook until browned, breaking it up into small pieces with a spoon. Drain excess fat if needed.
  5. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, pepper, and sugar (if using). Add water if the sauce is too thick.
  6. Lower the heat and let the sauce simmer for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow flavors to meld.

2. Prepare the Cheese Filling

  1. In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, Parmesan cheese, salt, pepper, and parsley. Mix well until smooth.

3. Preheat the Oven

  1. Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).

4. Assemble the Lasagna

  1. Spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Lay 3–4 lasagna sheets over the sauce to cover the base.
  3. Spread 1/3 of the ricotta mixture over the pasta sheets.
  4. Add 1/3 of the meat sauce on top, spreading evenly.
  5. Sprinkle 1/3 of the shredded mozzarella over the sauce.
  6. Repeat the layering process two more times: pasta, ricotta, meat sauce, mozzarella.
  7. Finish with a final layer of lasagna sheets topped with remaining sauce, mozzarella, and Parmesan cheese.

5. Bake the Lasagna

  1. Cover the dish with aluminium foil, ensuring it doesn't touch the cheese
  2. Bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes
  3. Remove the foil and bake for another 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.

6. Rest and Serve

  1. Let the lasagna rest for 10-15 minutes before cutting into portions. This helps it set and makes serving easier.
  2. Garnish with fresh basil (optional) and serve warm.

Enjoy your delicious homemade lasagna!

Wine Pairing Recommendations

Red Wine:

  • Chianti or Sangiovese: Bright acidity that cuts through the richness of lasagna.
  • Barbera or Zinfandel: Fruity profiles for a flavorful pairing.
  • Cabernet Sauvignon or Syrah/Shiraz: Adds depth, especially for smoky or spicy lasagna.

White Wine (for lighter or vegetarian lasagna):

  • Pinot Grigio or Chardonnay (unoaked): Crisp and refreshing.
  • Vermentino or Sauvignon Blanc: Offers a herbal and refreshing touch.

Dessert Recommendations

  • Tiramisu: A classic Italian dessert with espresso-soaked ladyfingers and mascarpone cream.
  • Panna Cotta: Silky and creamy, served with berry compote or caramel sauce.
  • Gelato or Sorbet:
    • Gelato: Flavors like pistachio, chocolate, or vanilla provide a rich contrast.
    • Sorbet: Lemon or raspberry for a light, palate-cleansing option.
  • Affogato: Vanilla gelato topped with a shot of hot espresso for a simple yet elegant treat.
