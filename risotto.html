Risotto alla Milanese

Description

Risotto alla Milanese is a classic Italian dish known for its creamy texture and vibrant golden hue. The key to its richness lies in the use of saffron, which is infused into the broth, giving the dish its signature flavor and color. Traditionally served in Milan, this risotto is made with Arborio rice, known for its ability to absorb liquids and create a creamy, velvety texture. The addition of butter, Parmesan cheese, and sometimes a touch of bone marrow or stock brings a richness that complements the delicate saffron. It’s a comforting yet refined dish, perfect for a special dinner or as a luxurious side to a main course.

Though it originates from Milan, Risotto alla Milanese has gained popularity across Italy and around the world. The balance of flavors makes it an ideal accompaniment to roasted meats or as a stand-alone dish. For those new to Italian cooking, it’s a great introduction to risotto, with its slightly sweet, floral saffron flavor providing a unique twist on the traditional risotto experience.

Ingredients

Here is an exact list of ingredients for Risotto alla Milanese that serves six people:

For the Broth

  • 1 liter (4 cups) chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads

For the Risotto

  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 300g (1 1/2 cups) Arborio rice
  • 150ml (1/2 cup) dry white wine
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter (for finishing)

Steps to Cook Risotto alla Milanese for 6 People

1. Prepare the Broth

  1. In a small saucepan, heat the chicken or vegetable broth over low heat.
  2. Add the saffron threads to the broth and let them steep as the broth warms up. This infuses the liquid with color and flavor.

2. Sauté the Onion

  1. In a large skillet or wide saucepan, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion and sauté for 3-4 minutes, until softened but not browned.

3. Toast the Rice

  1. Add the Arborio rice to the pan with the onions and stir to coat the grains in butter.
  2. Toast the rice for 1-2 minutes until it becomes translucent around the edges.

4. Add the Wine

  1. Pour in the white wine and cook, stirring constantly, until the wine is mostly absorbed by the rice.

5. Cook the Risotto

  1. Begin adding the saffron-infused broth to the rice, one ladle at a time, stirring constantly. Wait until most of the liquid is absorbed before adding more broth.
  2. Continue adding the broth, stirring, and letting the rice cook for about 18-20 minutes, until tender yet slightly al dente. The consistency should be creamy but not soupy.

6. Finish the Risotto

  1. Once the rice is cooked, stir in the grated Parmesan cheese and 2 tablespoons of butter to enrich the dish.
  2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

7. Serve

  1. Serve the risotto hot, garnished with additional Parmesan if desired.

Enjoy your luxurious Risotto alla Milanese!

Wine Pairing Recommendations

Red Wine:

  • Pinot Noir: Its light body and soft tannins complement the delicate saffron flavor of the risotto.
  • Chianti Classico: A fruity, medium-bodied red that pairs well with the creamy texture of the risotto.

White Wine:

  • Chardonnay (unoaked): A crisp, citrusy Chardonnay to balance the richness of the risotto.
  • Sauvignon Blanc: A zesty, herbal wine that works well with the saffron notes.

Dessert Recommendations

  • Tiramisu: A delightful coffee-flavored dessert that will end the meal on a sweet note.
  • Ricotta Cheesecake: A lighter, creamy alternative to traditional cheesecake with a hint of citrus.
  • Panna Cotta: A rich, creamy dessert that can be topped with fresh berries or caramel for added sweetness.
